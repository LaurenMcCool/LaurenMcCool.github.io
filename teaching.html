My Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy focuses on meeting students' individual and unique learning needs to ensure every learner feels supported and valued. I believe in creating a classroom that balances structure and flexibility; where students know what is expected of them, but also have the space to think, reflect, and grow in ways that suit their learning style.

I draw on both behavioural and cognitive behavioural approaches, which means I focus not only on clear expectations and routines, but also on helping students understand how they learn, manage their emotions, and build confidence in their abilities.

In practice, this means setting up a safe and predictable learning environment where students feel comfortable taking risks and being themselves. Structure gives students security, especially in hands-on subjects like TAS where safety is essential, but I also encourage them to reflect on their choices and learning so they can develop independence and resilience.

I aim to use positive strategies that engage students, while giving them the tools to manage their focus, motivation, and relationships. By showing empathy, maintaining fair but firm boundaries, and creating a classroom culture built on respect, I can support students not just as learners, but as people developing their own sense of self.

Creating a Positive Learning Environment

As a teacher, I aim to create a classroom where every student feels safe, valued, and included. I feel that learning happens best when students are not stressed and feel supported, so I work to build a positive classroom culture that encourages respect, empathy, and collaboration.

I strive to create a calming and welcoming atmosphere for all through the physical layout of the room, the way lessons are structured, atmospheric music or the tone I set in my interactions with students.

When students feel comfortable, they are more open to engaging, taking risks, and working through challenges.

Ultimately, my goal is to inspire a classroom culture where students feel safe to learn, motivated to grow, and confident in their abilities, knowing they have the support they need to succeed.
